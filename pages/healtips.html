COMMON EMERGENCIES: WHAT TO DO

Heatstroke

Move to a shaded or air-conditioned room.

Remove excess clothing.

Cool the person with wet cloths or a bath.

Call emergency services.

Heart Attack

Call emergency services.

Help the person sit comfortably.

Provide aspirin if available (unless allergic).

Begin CPR if unresponsive and not breathing.

Sudden Fainting

Lay the person down and elevate legs.

Check breathing and pulse.

Loosen tight clothing.

Seek medical help if unconscious for more than 2-3 minutes.

DISASTER READY? READY!

Earthquake

  • Drop, cover, and hold under sturdy furniture.
  • Stay away from windows and outside walls.
  • Check for injuries and evacuate if necessary.

Tsunami

  • If you feel a strong coastal earthquake, evacuate immediately.
  • Do not wait for a warning siren.
  • Stay tuned to emergency radios or official sources.

Flash Flooding

  • Move to higher ground without delay.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters — 6 inches can knock you down.
  • Listen to weather alerts and evacuation notices.

LIFESTYLE TIPS TO TAKE CARE OF YOURSELF

Daily Health Tips

  • Hydrate: Aim for 6–8 glasses of water a day.
  • Get 7–9 hours of sleep.
  • Eat balanced meals, including fruits & veggies.
  • Exercise at least 30 mins a day.

Mental Wellness

  • Talk about your feelings.
  • Take breaks and get enough rest.
  • Reach out if you’re feeling sad or anxious.
